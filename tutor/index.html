Background & Experience

Schooling

I received my B.S in Computer Science with a specialization in algorithms from the University of California Irvine (UCI) in 2018. I can email you a copy of my transcript upon request.

Python lab tutor for UCI freshman

When I was a sophomore at UCI I volunteered to be a tutor for the introductory series on programming in Python. Three time a week I would go to a programming lab and help students with their projects. I maintained this commitment until graduating.

Girls Who Code

In my junior year I began volunteering for an organization called Girls Who Code. My audience was a group of middle school girls with limited experience programming. In terms of teaching, this was a new challenge for me. I learned how to explain programming in plain english and through analogies. I continued volunteering for Girls Who Code through 2020. Due to covid-19, we went from an in person class to an online class. I hope to volunteer with them in the future.

The Coder School

After college, I worked in the industry for a year but was not feeling fulfilled. I decided to change my career path and focus on tutoring computer science. I was able to find a job at The Coder School in Irvine where I worked at for over a year. While there, I taught students from elementary to high school programming. I spent roughly 3/4 of the time teaching in person and 1/4 via zoom. One of the things I respect about The Coder School is that they encourage tutors to teach their own curriculum and personalize it to the student. This is an approach I maintain in my own private tutoring. As much as I enjoyed working there, it was not feasible for me to maintain my living expenses while working there. So, I decided to start tutoring privately.

Private Tutoring (2019 - Current)

Since leaving The Coder School I have been able to continue tutoring on my own through Zoom. It has been lots of fun and everyday I feel excited to work with my students.
